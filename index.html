Evidence-Based Treatments

Prolonged Exposure (PE)

Gold Standard

Gradually approaching trauma-related memories and situations in a safe environment, reducing their power over time. Highly effective with strong research support.

Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT)

Gold Standard

Identifies and challenges unhelpful beliefs developed after trauma ("stuck points"). Typically 12 sessions. Effective for various trauma types.

EMDR

Recommended

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing uses bilateral stimulation while processing traumatic memories. Well-researched and effective for many.

Medications

Adjunct

SSRIs (sertraline, paroxetine) are FDA-approved for PTSD. Prazosin helps with nightmares. Medications often combined with therapy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What kinds of events cause PTSD?

PTSD can develop after experiencing or witnessing actual or threatened death, serious injury, or sexual violence. This includes combat, assault, accidents, natural disasters, childhood abuse, and other traumatic events. Not everyone who experiences trauma develops PTSD.

How long do symptoms need to last for a PTSD diagnosis?

Symptoms must persist for more than one month and cause significant distress or impairment. Many people experience acute stress symptoms after trauma that resolve on their own. If symptoms last beyond a month, professional evaluation is recommended.

Can PTSD develop years after the trauma?

Yes. While symptoms often begin within 3 months of trauma, "delayed-onset PTSD" can emerge months or even years later, sometimes triggered by life changes, additional stress, or reminders of the original trauma.

Does treatment really work?

Yes. Research shows 53-80% of people completing trauma-focused therapy no longer meet PTSD criteria afterward. Treatment doesn't erase memories but changes how your brain processes them, reducing their distressing impact.

Will I have to talk about what happened in detail?

Effective treatments do involve processing the trauma, but this happens gradually at your pace with a trained therapist. You're never forced to share more than you're ready for. The goal is to reduceâ€”not increaseâ€”distress over time.
